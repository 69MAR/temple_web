Special Sevas (On Request)

Ranga Pooje As per request Special

Ranga Pooje is a special evening ritual performed only on the request of devotees. It begins at 6:00 PM, where freshly cooked rice is first offered to Lord Mahalingeshwara and Lord Ganapathi. Following this, the Bali ritual is carried out, where uncooked rice placed on leaves is respectfully offered to the Bali stones located both inside and outside the temple premises.

The glow of deepas, the fragrance of flowers, and the chants during this seva create a truly divine atmosphere. Ranga Pooje symbolizes nourishment, protection, and the continuous blessings of the deities upon the devotees and the community.

Shatarudrabhisheka As per request Special

Shatarudrabhisheka is regarded as one of the most powerful and sacred sevas offered to Lord Mahalingeshwara. In this ritual, the Sri Rudram is chanted one hundred times with deep devotion while the Shiva Linga is bathed with holy water, panchamruta (milk, curd, ghee, honey and sugar), tender coconut, and other sacred offerings.

The entire temple resonates with the sound of the Vedas during this seva. The presence of devotees, the continuous flow of abhisheka, and the chanting of mantras together create a divine atmosphere that uplifts the mind and fills the heart with peace.

It is believed that performing Shatarudrabhisheka helps in removing obstacles, protecting the family, granting good health, and bringing prosperity. This seva requires several priests and is performed only on request with prior booking.

Many devotees choose to perform Shatarudrabhisheka during special occasions such as Shravan masa, on Monday, or to mark personal milestones and sankalpas. With sincere faith, this offering brings the blessings of Lord Mahalingeshwara in abundance.
